# Agile, Jira, and Kanban Workflow

## Objective

This document summarizes my understanding and hands-on exposure to Agile-style business analysis workflows, Jira-based tracking, and Kanban-style work management.

## Business Context

In enterprise projects, Business Analysts often work with product owners, developers, QA teams, business stakeholders, and support teams. Clear tracking of requirements, issues, priorities, and progress is important for successful delivery.

## Agile / Jira Workflow

Backlog Item Created  
↓  
Requirement Reviewed  
↓  
User Story Defined  
↓  
Acceptance Criteria Added  
↓  
Development / Configuration  
↓  
Testing / UAT  
↓  
Stakeholder Review  
↓  
Completed / Deployed  

## Kanban Workflow

To Do  
↓  
In Progress  
↓  
Blocked / Review  
↓  
Testing  
↓  
Done  

## Business Analyst Activities

- Break business needs into user stories
- Add acceptance criteria
- Prioritize backlog items
- Track blockers
- Coordinate with technical teams
- Support UAT
- Communicate status to stakeholders
- Identify process improvement opportunities

## Example User Story

As a sales manager,  
I want a dashboard showing lead and opportunity status,  
So that I can monitor pipeline progress and identify follow-up gaps.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Dashboard shows open leads
- Dashboard shows opportunity stage
- Users can filter by owner, status, and date range
- Report supports weekly review meetings

## Business Value

Agile and Kanban workflows improve transparency, prioritization, team coordination, delivery tracking, and stakeholder alignment.
