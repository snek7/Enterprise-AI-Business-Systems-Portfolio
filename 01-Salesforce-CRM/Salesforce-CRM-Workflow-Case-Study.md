# Salesforce CRM Case Study: Lead-to-Quote Workflow

## Objective

This case study documents my hands-on exposure to Salesforce Sales Cloud workflows from a Business Analyst perspective, focusing on lead tracking, opportunity visibility, quote-related activities, and reporting.

## Business Context

Sales and customer-facing teams need a structured CRM process to manage incoming leads, qualify prospects, track opportunities, support quote-related activities, and monitor follow-up actions.

A well-managed CRM workflow improves pipeline visibility, customer engagement, reporting accuracy, and operational coordination.

## Workflow Covered

Lead Created  
↓  
Lead Qualification  
↓  
Opportunity Review  
↓  
Quote-Related Activity  
↓  
Customer Follow-Up  
↓  
Pipeline / Reporting Visibility  

## Salesforce Areas Covered

- Sales App Home
- Leads
- Opportunities
- Accounts
- Reports
- Dashboards / sales visibility

## Business Analyst Activities Demonstrated

- Reviewed the lead-to-opportunity workflow
- Identified key CRM objects involved in the sales process
- Documented business process flow from lead intake to opportunity tracking
- Connected CRM activities with reporting and dashboard visibility
- Translated workflow understanding into user stories, acceptance criteria, and KPIs
- Maintained data privacy by using a Salesforce practice/developer environment and avoiding confidential customer data

## Sample User Stories

### User Story 1

As a sales representative,  
I want to track lead status,  
So that I can prioritize follow-up activities and avoid missing potential customers.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Lead status should be visible on the lead record
- Leads should be filterable by status
- Follow-up actions should be trackable
- Sales users should be able to identify open leads

### User Story 2

As a sales manager,  
I want visibility into opportunities,  
So that I can monitor pipeline progress and identify deals that need attention.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Opportunities should show stage and status
- Pipeline value should be visible
- Reports should summarize open opportunities
- Managers should be able to identify stalled opportunities

### User Story 3

As a business analyst,  
I want Salesforce reports on lead and opportunity activity,  
So that I can identify process bottlenecks and recommend improvements.

### Acceptance Criteria

- Reports should show lead volume
- Reports should show opportunity status
- Reports should support filtering by owner, stage, or time period
- Dashboard views should support business decision-making

## Reporting KPIs

- Total Leads
- Qualified Leads
- Open Opportunities
- Opportunity Conversion Rate
- Quote / Follow-up Status
- Pipeline Value
- Average Follow-up Time
- Closed Won Opportunities

## Business Value

This workflow supports better sales visibility, improved customer follow-up, stronger pipeline tracking, more accurate reporting, and better decision-making for sales and business teams.
