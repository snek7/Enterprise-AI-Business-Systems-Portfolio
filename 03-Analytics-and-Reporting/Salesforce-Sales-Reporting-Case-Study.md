# Salesforce Sales Reporting Case Study

## Objective

This case study demonstrates how Salesforce reporting can support sales visibility, lead tracking, opportunity management, and business decision-making.

## Business Context

Sales and business teams need clear visibility into lead volume, opportunity progress, quote-related activity, pipeline health, and follow-up actions. Without structured reporting, managers may struggle to identify bottlenecks, delayed follow-ups, or pipeline risks.

## Reporting Areas Covered

- Lead tracking
- Opportunity pipeline visibility
- Quote and follow-up monitoring
- Sales activity tracking
- Executive reporting
- Dashboard-based decision support

## Key KPIs

- Total Leads
- Qualified Leads
- Lead Conversion Rate
- Open Opportunities
- Opportunity Win Rate
- Pipeline Value
- Quote / Follow-up Status
- Average Follow-up Time
- Closed Won Revenue

## Sample Dashboard Requirements

The dashboard should allow business users to:

- View open leads by status
- Track opportunities by stage
- Monitor quote-related follow-up activity
- Identify stalled opportunities
- Review pipeline value
- Track conversion performance
- Support weekly sales review meetings

## Business Analyst Contribution

As a Business Analyst, I would support this reporting workflow by:

- Gathering reporting requirements from sales stakeholders
- Defining KPI logic and business rules
- Identifying required Salesforce fields
- Working with technical teams to build reports/dashboards
- Supporting UAT and stakeholder validation
- Translating dashboard insights into process improvement recommendations

## Business Value

A strong Salesforce reporting framework improves sales visibility, follow-up discipline, pipeline transparency, and leadership decision-making.
